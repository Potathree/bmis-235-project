My (unordered) Top Ten Games

  1. Stardew Valley
    • It's an incredibly "Cozy" Experience, and brings a warm smile to my lips whenever I play it.
    • Despite being nearly 10 years old at this point, it still recives updates, and there are plans for future updates, keeping the game in my mind and giving value to going back and replaying the game.
  2. Katana ZERO
    • Some games can be described as just fun- this is one of them.
    • This game is constant, edge to edge action, from one scene to the next, there is no stopping. It's a game that tells you to keep fighting, and makes it addictive to keep on doing so.
  3. Disco Elysium
    • You wake up in a trashed room, as an alcoholic, amnesiac detective. It's your job to solve a murder, a murder of which there are no details that you can rememeber.
    • If there was any game on this list I had to encourge anyone to try, it would be this one. It's not for everyone, but a game everyone should try. It's a game which made me laugh, cry, and sit with myself uncomfortably.
  4. Dungeons and Dragons
    • A game who's quality is determined by who you play it with, but which has some of the highest highs of any game.
    • Certainly the most time consuming of any of the games on this list, but one which I find very rewarding in turn. If anyone gets the chance to try it, I would encourage them to dip their toes into it.
  5. Barotrauma
    • You are in a submarine, in the seas of Europa, of the moons of Jupiter. The world outside is deeply hostile to any life, and you must survive the harsh conditions of the moon.
    • An incredibly stressful game, wherein you spend most of your time patching up a slowly failing submarine, as creatures from the depths of Europa attempt to eat you. The constant stress makes it a game filled with tension, making it one of my favorites for how much it makes me on edge.
  6. Armored Core VI: Fires of Rubicon
    • You are a mercenary in a mechsuit- your job is to follow the orders of your commander like a dog, and earn money for them until you eventually croak, until you start hearing a voice in your head.
    • Incredibly high paced action, along with a story that is gripping with chocies that have long and far reaching consequences. Do you remain a loyal dog of the corperations, who are draining and killing the planet for the 'coral' which powers your mech suits, or side with the liberation front when you can, knowing that though they have good intentions, it won't pad your bottom line. A very well balanced game which left me thinking and excited for more.
  7. The House in Fata Morgana
    • Visual Novels, like this one, are hard to get into for the average person who likes games, and for the average person who likes novels. To me, it's one of the greatest ways to experience the medium of a novel, with beautiful images to encourage me on, though I have a passion for reading anyways, and a soundtrack that is haunting. Still if you have the time and are willing to put in the effort, The House in Fata Morgana is a story which will reward you as you read a story about stories, spanning a thousand years and yet feeling personal and beautiful every step of the way.
    • A wonderfully told story about the stories of those who are called good and evil, and how they came to be, and which leaves you looking at them in disgust and hope. Ugly and beautiful all the same.
  8. Metal Gear Solid V
    • You are a soldier of fortune, the best of the best, known as the Big Boss. After a terrible accident, you are given the task of being back up your empire, as you discover a conspiracy that could destroy the western world.
    • This game feels unfinished. The gameplay is polished, but there are parts in the story which feel like it is wholy undone, and that if the writer was given just a few more months to work on it, that it would shine even more. I think it speaks to the quality of everything else, and to the story which remains, that it's still on this list.
  9. Slay the Princess
    • You're on a path in the woods, and at the end of that path is a cabin. And in the basement of that cabin is a Princess. You're here to slay her. If you don't, it will be the end of the world. She will do everything in her power to stop you. She'll charm, and she'll lie, and she'll promise you the world, and if you let her, she'll kill you a dozen times over. You can't let that happen. You do care about the fate of the world, right?
    • An incredibly meta story that captivated me from the start. And incredibly well told story, which is charming, gripping, and horrifying all the same.
  10. Hollow Knight
    • Adventure through a charming kingdom of bugs and discover what lies beneath.
    • There isn't much in the hook for this game. In my mind, it's quite simple, the story is quality with impacting actions, and the gameplay is polished. To me, that's it's appeal. Polish. This is a game with a strong concept, which has been executed incredibly. To say any more would spoil it, and degrade it.
